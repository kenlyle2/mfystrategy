## 🟢 WHAT'S WORKING FOR THEM (Threat / Model)

### 1. Flex Friday is a cultural engine

Three of the top 10 posts are Flex Friday content:
- Jan 16: "Is flexing in the mirror still cool? #flexfriday" → **0.87%**
- Jan 9: "Flex Friday - training between assembling toys..." → **0.57%**
- Feb 6: "#FlexFriday Yes I see you hating side plank" → **0.31%**

The hashtag #FlexFriday averages **0.44% engagement** — more than double the account average. It's a recurring ritual that his audience shows up for.

**So what:** ZeroDollarBlueprint should establish its own recurring Friday ritual that resonates with the longevity / financial mortality angle. Something like "Longevity Friday" or "Zero Dollar Friday" — a consistent hook that trains the algorithm and the audience simultaneously.

### 2. Personal vulnerability outperforms everything

The #1 post of the entire 90-day period:
> "This was fun! Here's the prompt: Create a caricature of me and my job..." → **1.26% engagement, 587 likes, 33 comments**

#2 post: "So it might not all go according to plan — there's challenges I've encountered..." → **0.90%**

#3: "When's the last time you did something just for fun? I'm on the ski lift at Keystone with my 5-year-old..." → **0.73%**

The pattern: **Dean being human, imperfect, or playful crushes any workout tutorial.** The audience follows the person, not the program.

**So what:** ZeroDollarBlueprint's angle — "what Bryan Johnson does for $2M, I do for free" — is inherently personal and contrarian. Lean into that voice. The "I tried the expensive thing and found a cheaper way" frame is authentic and highly shareable.

### 3. Erectile dysfunction content outperforms its volume

ED-tagged content clusters around 0.22–0.78% engagement — consistently above average. The audience responds to taboo health topics because the barrier to talk about them elsewhere is high. Man Flow Yoga has created a safe space for this.

**Implication for ZeroDollarBlueprint:** Longevity content has its own taboo-adjacent territory — mortality anxiety, declining performance, "why am I aging faster than I should." Content that names what men don't talk about likely has the same outsized engagement dynamics.

### 4. Real-time events spike engagement

Austin Marathon Expo posts:
- "We're all set up and ready!" → **0.74%**
- "Had a great time at austinmarathon expo" → **0.38%**
- "Throwback to Austin Marathon" (TBT) → **0.29%**

Being *in the world* and reporting back performs well. The #austinmarathon hashtag averaged 0.42% across 3 posts.

## 🎯 ZeroDollarBlueprint Competitive Angles

Based on where Dean is weakest and what's working:

1. **Lead with carousels.** His carousel engagement per post is 3.2× his video. If you match him on carousel volume, you can beat his per-post engagement while creating less content.

2. **Own Wednesday and Friday.** These are his best days. Compete on his best days, not his dead ones.

3. **Be personal first, educational second.** His top posts are person-led. Tutorials and pain relief content is his bread and butter but his worst-performing category.

4. **Name the things men don't talk about.** ED, aging faster than peers, financial mortality — the ZeroDollarBlueprint angle is inherently taboo-adjacent. That's where his audience engages most.

5. **Don't try to out-hashtag him.** His own most-used hashtags are below his average. Fresh, specific hashtags (events, topics, communities) outperform the saturated branded ones.
